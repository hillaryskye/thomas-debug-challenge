In 1992 I graduated with an MBA from the University of Chicago. No employer hired me because I stuttered severely. Seven speech therapy programs had little or no effect on my speech.

I went to the university library and read every book and journal about stuttering. I found some studies of laboratory experiments in which electronic devices made stutterers talk fluently. These devices weren't available for sale so I built one. Then my friends in my stuttering support group asked me to build devices for them.

I founded Casa Futura Technologies and for more than twenty years I've made technology for treating stuttering, Parkinson's, and other speech disorders.

Three years ago revenues fell off a cliff. I've tried to turn the company around but three or four trends are against it. It's time to do something different. Coding and building websites has been my favorite part of my work twenty years, so…
